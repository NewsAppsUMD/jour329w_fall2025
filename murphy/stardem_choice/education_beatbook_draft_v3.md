The 2024–25 MCAP test results, released in August 2025, exposed stark academic disparities across Queen Anne’s County. Dorchester County schools saw a 3-point proficiency rise in math, while Caroline County plummeted 2 points, and Kent County flatlined. Superintendents Patricia Saelens and Mary Boswell-McComas have since prioritized “targeted interventions,” but the April 2025 State of Reading Report—showing a decade-long decline in literacy—cements education leaders’ fears that pandemic recovery is uneven and incomplete. These findings come amid a 30% spike in student homelessness reported in July 2025 by Talbot and Kent Counties, compounding the challenges educators face in addressing both academic and socio-economic gaps.  

Historical data provides context for these struggles. In November 2023, MCAP results signaled pandemic learning loss, but recent outcomes indicate lingering instability. The March 2025 Blueprint discussions highlighted extended school days and tutoring as partial solutions, yet districts like Queen Anne’s continue contending with shifting demographics and funding constraints. For example, opioid restitution funds allocated to health education in August 2025 reflect a broader push to address root causes of underperformance, tying student achievement to community-level interventions. However, as Talbot County’s analysis of discipline trends in October 2025 links poor school climate to low academic outcomes, it’s clear that solutions require a multi-faceted approach.  

The urgency of these issues is further amplified by advocacy efforts. Maryland READS Executive Director Trish Brennan-Gac has repeatedly warned lawmakers that without accelerated literacy reforms, the state risks falling further behind national benchmarks. Meanwhile, local schools’ reliance on Blueprint funds to stabilize budgets—evident in April 2025 Chesapeake College tech center cost shifts—highlights the precarious balance between investing in infrastructure and direct student support. As districts race to adapt curricula and secure resources, the line between recovery and regression grows increasingly thin.

### **Budget & Finance Records**  
- **Annual School District Budgets**: Detail funding allocations for programs, payrolls, capital projects, and Blueprint for Maryland’s Future compliance.  
  - *Find*: County School Board websites (e.g., Talbot County Public Schools, Caroline County Public Schools) and MSDE’s Fiscal Operations Portal.  
- **Cade Funding Formula Reports**: Track state and county contributions to education, identifying equity gaps and funding disparities.  
  - *Find*: Maryland State Department of Education (MSDE) Funding Analysis Reports and county fiscal offices.  
- **Blueprint for Maryland’s Future Funding Reports**: Document state and local investments in full-day pre-K, smaller class sizes, and social-emotional learning.  
  - *Find*: Blueprint Accountability & Implementation Board Public Dashboards and county school board meeting minutes.  
- **County Budget Proposals and Requests**: Reflect local contributions to school funding (e.g., Talbot County Council’s 2025–26 request).  
  - *Find*: County Council agendas and approved budgets on local government websites.  
- **Quarterly Financial Statements**: Provide updates on revenue, expenses, and reserve balances for school districts.
