# Immigration Reporter's Guide: Maryland and the Washington Region

## Overview

Immigration has become one of the most contentious and rapidly evolving policy areas affecting Maryland and the broader Washington region. With nearly 1 million immigrants in Maryland (about 15% of the state's population) and significant policy shifts under different administrations, this beat requires understanding both national policy and local impact.

## Key People and Institutions

### Federal Officials
- **Department of Homeland Security (DHS)**: Oversees immigration enforcement, including ICE operations
- **U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS)**: Processes visa applications, DACA renewals, and naturalization cases

### Maryland Congressional Delegation
- **Sen. Chris Van Hollen (D)**: Consistently advocates for immigrant rights, has traveled to El Salvador to check on deportees
- **Sen. Ben Cardin (D)**: Co-sponsors immigration protection legislation
- **Rep. Steny Hoyer (D-Mechanicsville)**: House Majority Leader, supports pathway to citizenship
- **Rep. Andy Harris (R-Cockeysville)**: Generally supports stricter immigration enforcement

### State and Local Officials
- **Gov. Wes Moore (D)**: Welcomed Afghan evacuees, supports immigrant communities
- **Maryland Attorney General Brian Frosh**: Filed lawsuits against Trump-era immigration policies
- **Catalina Rodriguez Lima**: Director, Baltimore Mayor's Office of Immigrant Affairs

### Advocacy Organizations
- **CASA de Maryland** (Langley Park headquarters): Largest Latino/immigrant advocacy organization in the Mid-Atlantic
  - Provides legal services, DACA assistance, worker protections
  - Key contacts: Gustavo Torres (Executive Director), Cathryn Paul (Research/Policy Analyst)
  
- **Lutheran Immigration and Refugee Service** (Baltimore): Major refugee resettlement organization
  - CEO: Krish O'Mara Vignarajah
  
- **Centro de los Derechos del Migrante**: Advocates for migrant worker rights

- **International Rescue Committee** (Baltimore office)
- **Episcopal Refugee and Immigrant Center Alliance**

### Academic Experts
- **Christina Getrich**: UMD professor, anthropologist studying immigration
- **Elizabeth Keyes**: Assistant law professor, Director of Immigrants Rights Clinic, University of Baltimore
- **Mike Amezcua**: Assistant professor of history, Georgetown University

## Geographic Focus Areas

### High-Immigrant Communities
- **Langley Park (Prince George's County)**: 83% Hispanic population, major day laborer market
- **Montgomery County**: About 30% immigrant population, largest in state
- **Baltimore**: Accepts nearly half of Maryland's refugees; significant Jamaican community
- **Eastern Shore**: Relies heavily on H-2B visa workers for seafood processing

## Current and Ongoing Issues

### Legal Limbo Cases (URGENT - UNRESOLVED)

**Kilmar Abrego García Case**: This is **ongoing litigation** with unclear resolution status. A Maryland resident was wrongfully deported to El Salvador's CECOT prison despite court orders:
- Supreme Court ruled 9-0 that government must facilitate his return
- As of April 2025, Trump administration and El Salvador's President Bukele both refused to bring him back
- Sen. Van Hollen visited him in El Salvador
- Federal judges threatened contempt proceedings
- **Follow-up needed**: Check current status of this case

### Visa and Work Authorization Programs

**H-2B Visa Program** (Seafood/Hospitality Workers):
- Maryland seafood industry received 2,232 visas (2019-2020)
- Eastern Shore crab processors heavily dependent on these workers
- Ongoing concerns about:
  - COVID-19 safety (workers travel long distances by bus)
  - Wage issues
  - Housing conditions
  - **Story angle**: Limited federal/state protections remain despite pandemic

**J-1 Visa Program** (Seasonal Workers):
- Ocean City employs about 4,000 J-1 students each summer
- Cultural exchange program crucial for resort operations
- **Status unclear**: Check if Trump-era reduction proposals were implemented

### DACA (Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals)

**Current status as of 2025**: Unclear whether Biden administration has secured permanent protections
- About 9,700 Maryland DACA recipients (as of 2023 data)
- December 2021 court ruling ordered DHS to accept new applications again
- **Follow-up needed**: Has Congress passed permanent DREAM Act legislation?

### Birthright Citizenship Challenge

**January 2025**: Biden joined 15+ states in lawsuit against Trump executive order attempting to end birthright citizenship
- Supreme Court case pending (as of May 2025 articles)
- **Critical follow-up**: What was the Supreme Court's ruling?

### Afghan Refugee Resettlement

Maryland received 1,348 Afghan evacuees following 2021 withdrawal
- Temporary legal status through humanitarian parole
- Advocates pushed for Afghan Adjustment Act (status unclear in 2025)
- **Story angles**: 
  - How are families faring 3+ years later?
  - Did Afghan Adjustment Act pass?
  - Employment/integration challenges

### ICE Workplace Raids

**May 2025**: DHS visited 100+ businesses in DC area, including popular restaurants:
- Served Notice of Inspections for I-9 audits
- No warrants required under current law
- Restaurants affected: Pupatella, Chef Geoff's, Call Your Mother, Clyde's, others
- **Ongoing concern**: Chilling effect on immigrant communities reporting crimes

### Refugee Admissions

Trump administration cut refugee ceiling to 18,000 (2020), lowest since 1980
- **Biden administration**: Check current ceiling and Maryland's acceptance numbers
- Baltimore has accepted nearly half of Maryland's refugees since 2000

## Story Angles to Pursue

### Immediate Follow-Ups Needed

1. **Abrego García case resolution**: This is a major constitutional case with unclear outcome. Contact ACLU, CASA legal team, or his attorneys.

2. **Supreme Court birthright citizenship ruling**: Should have been decided by now. This affects pregnant women like "Meny" from the CASA v. Trump case.

3. **Afghan Adjustment Act**: Did it pass? How are the 1,348+ Maryland Afghan evacuees doing?

4. **DACA permanent solution**: Has Congress acted since December 2021 court ruling?

5. **ICE workplace raids aftermath**: Follow up with May 2025 restaurants—were workers deported? Did families separate?

## Important Context

### Legal Framework
- **287(g) agreements**: Allow local officers to act as federal immigration agents
- **Intergovernmental Service Agreements**: Counties rent jail space to ICE
- **Temporary Protected Status (TPS)**: Protects people from unsafe countries
- **Special Immigrant Visas (SIV)**: For Afghan/Iraqi allies who worked with U.S.

### Language Matters
- Federal law uses "alien"—many find this dehumanizing
- "Undocumented" vs. "illegal" immigrant—be precise and neutral
- "Dreamers"—DACA recipients
- "Mixed-status families"—some members documented, others not

### Common Misconceptions to Clarify
- Crossing border without authorization = misdemeanor
- Overstaying visa = civil infraction (not criminal)
- Studies show immigrants (including undocumented) have lower crime rates
- Many DACA recipients were brought as infants/toddlers

## Red Flags and Verification Needs

- **Claims about immigrant crime**: Always check against actual data
- **Deportation numbers**: Verify whether Trump or Biden administration had higher numbers (data shows Biden actually deported more in some periods)
- **Economic impact claims**: Check whether studies account for taxes paid vs. services used
- **"Sanctuary city" status**: Verify actual policies, not just rhetoric

## Ethical Considerations

1. **Protecting Sources**: Use pseudonyms, avoid identifying details for undocumented immigrants
2. **Family Separation**: Be sensitive when interviewing children or separated families
3. **Trauma**: Many immigrants have experienced violence; approach interviews carefully
4. **Legal Jeopardy**: Be aware that information you publish could be used by ICE
5. **Balance**: Include voices of those concerned about immigration alongside advocates

## Quick Reference: Maryland Immigration by Numbers

- **Total immigrants**: ~1 million (15% of population)
- **Undocumented**: ~275,000 (30% of immigrant population)
- **DACA recipients**: ~9,700 (as of 2023)
- **Top countries of origin**: El Salvador, India, China, Philippines, Jamaica
- **Refugees**: Baltimore accepts nearly 50% of Maryland's refugees
- **Economic contribution**: $1.2+ billion in state/local taxes (2022)

This beat requires building trust with vulnerable communities while pursuing accountability from institutions. The legal landscape changes rapidly—court rulings, policy shifts, and enforcement priorities can transform overnight. Cultivate sources on all sides, verify everything, and always consider both the human impact and the broader policy implications of your reporting.
